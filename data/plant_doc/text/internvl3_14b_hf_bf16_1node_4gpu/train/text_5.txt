The image shows a pear tree with a leaf that has numerous small, round, reddish-brown spots. These spots are likely indicative of a fungal infection, possibly pear scab (Venturia nashicola) or a similar disease. Pear scab typically appears as dark, velvety spots on leaves and fruit. It's important to manage this issue to prevent further spread and damage to the tree. Here are some steps you can take:

1. **Remove Infected Leaves and Fruit:** Prune and dispose of any infected leaves and fruit to reduce the spread of the fungus.
2. **Improve Air Circulation:** Thin out the canopy to improve air flow, which can help prevent fungal growth.
3. **Fungicide Application:** Apply a fungicide, following the instructions on the label, to protect the tree from further infection.
4. **Water Management:** Avoid overhead watering, as this can spread the fungus. Water at the base of the tree instead.
5. **Choose Resistant Varieties:** If the infection persists, consider planting resistant varieties of pears in the future.
